01 — The Uncomfortable Truth

They Don't Want a Job.
They Want Security.

This is the most important insight in this entire document. Your audience isn't just job hunting — they're chasing a feeling. A government job in NE India represents the end of anxiety. It means permanent income, social status, pension, parental approval, and freedom from daily uncertainty. Every piece of content you create must speak to this deeper emotional truth — not just the surface-level job posting.

"Agar sarkari naukri lag jaye toh life set ho jaye."

— The sentence playing on a loop in your audience's head

Understand this and every messaging decision becomes obvious. You're not selling job listings. You're selling hope made actionable.

03 — Emotional Trigger Map

The 12 Emotions That
Drive Every Action

Great content doesn't inform — it triggers. Here are the exact emotional levers that make your audience stop scrolling, save, share, and click.

😱

Fear of Missing Out (FOMO)

"⚠️ Last 48 hours — Assam Police recruitment closes Sunday." This is your highest-performing emotional trigger. Use it on every expiring job. Urgency = action.

🌟

Aspiration & Hope

"From her small village in Barpeta, she cracked APSC." Success stories activate hope. Hope activates sharing. Sharing activates growth. Use this weekly.

😤

Frustration with Chaos

"Tired of 15 tabs open just to track one job?" You're the antidote to chaos. Call out the pain explicitly. Your clean, no-ad experience is the solution.

💪

Validation & Recognition

"You're preparing while everyone else has already given up." Acknowledge their grind. Aspirants who've been trying for 3+ years HUNGER for someone to see their effort.

🧭

Need for Clarity

"Exactly which documents do you need for this application — in order." Step-by-step content makes you the trusted guide in a confusing world. Massive save and share rates.

⏳

Time & Age Anxiety

"You have 3 years before age bar. Here's what to do NOW." Many APSC aspirants watch their age limit approaching. This creates powerful urgency without manipulation.

🏡

Family Pressure & Guilt

Never exploit this — but acknowledge it. "Your parents want the best for you. Here's how to make them proud with [Job Name]." Reframe guilt into motivation.

🏆

Social Comparison

"2,340 people from NE have already applied to this job." Social proof + mild comparison triggers action. People move when they see peers moving.

🔍

Curiosity

"This grade-III job pays ₹42,000/month — and most people don't know it exists." Pattern interrupt. Curiosity gaps get clicks, shares, and saves more than anything else.

🚫

Fear of Being Deceived

"We never post unofficial listings. Every job is verified." Trust signals are emotional triggers too. In a space full of scams, your reliability is emotional relief.

💰

Salary & Benefits Desire

"Grade IV salary breakdown: ₹14,000 basic + ₹7,200 DA + ₹3,200 HRA = ₹24,400/month + pension." Concrete numbers perform incredibly well. People will screenshot and share.

🤯

Information Overwhelm

"I'll do the tracking so you don't have to." Your USPs (filters, alerts, clean UI) directly solve this. Make content that demonstrates the relief of using your platform.

04 — The Two Platform Universes

Facebook & Instagram
Are Different Worlds

This is where most brand pages fail — they post the same content everywhere and wonder why it doesn't work. You've correctly identified that these platforms serve fundamentally different audiences with different psychology. Here's the complete breakdown.

Facebook
The Normie Boomer Universe
Text-first Sharing culture 35+ audience

Facebook in NE India is where parents, teachers, and older siblings live. They are trust-first, share-happy, and scroll slowly. They read full posts. They comment. They forward to family WhatsApp groups. Win them and they become your distribution engine.

🏆 What Actually Works Here

Long-form text posts with emotional opening lines. "আজি এটা সুখবৰ" (Good news today) style openers. Job alerts with full details written out — salary, qualifications, deadline, link. No link-only posts.

👨‍👩‍👧 The Parent Forward Hack

End every job post with: "আপোনাৰ সন্তানক শ্বেয়াৰ কৰক" (Share with your children). Parents will forward your posts to every family group. This is free virality.

📝 Post Format That Performs

Opening hook line (emotional) → Job name + key details in bullet points → Eligibility clearly stated → Deadline in bold → Link to apply → CTA to share. Keep it scannable.

🚫 What to Avoid on Facebook

Short cryptic posts. Posts that just say "Link in bio." Instagram-style aesthetic posts with minimal text. Memes (wrong audience). Anything that requires effort to understand.

Instagram
The Mixed-Gen Scroll Machine
Reels-first Visual hooks 18–32 audience

Instagram is where your 18–28 audience dominates, but you've noticed something real: reels with girls get more views, and reasoning/quiz-type content performs well. Both of these are completely explainable — and exploitable.

👩 Why the "Girl Factor" Works on IG

It's not just about gender — it's about relatability and aspiration combined. A girl from NE India in uniform (or on her selection day) is the most powerful image you can post. She looks like your audience. She becomes proof of possibility. Use real user success stories featuring women — with permission. Never fake it.

🧠 Why Reasoning Questions Explode

They trigger a cognitive engagement loop. Person sees question → thinks → watches to end for answer → comments → shares to test friends. Algorithm sees high watch time + comments = pushes reel to 10x more people. It's a perfectly engineered engagement machine.

⚡ Reel Hook Formula for Your Niche

First 1.5 seconds must create a curiosity gap OR a challenge. "Can you answer what 90% of aspirants get wrong?" OR a face on screen saying "I got selected in Assam Police. Here's what nobody tells you." Then deliver the value fast.

📊 Carousel Saves Strategy

Carousels with "swipe to see which jobs are for you based on your qualification" perform well because they're personalized. End slide always: "Set alerts for your qualification type at necareerhub.com"

Instagram Reels — Specific Format Playbook

The 3 Reel Formats That Will Win for NE Career Hub

Based on what you've observed working in your niche + platform psychology. Execute these consistently.

Format 01 — "The Quiz Reel"

"Can you solve this?"

Show a reasoning question on screen (GK, Maths, verbal). "90% of APSC aspirants get this wrong →" Give 5 seconds. Reveal answer with explanation. End: "Follow for daily practice questions." → High watch time, high comments, high shares

Format 02 — "The Success Story Reel"

"She got selected at 24"

Real girl/boy from NE India, their photo or short clip, text overlay telling the story. "She had failed twice. Applied via NE Career Hub. Never missed an alert. Got selected." End with: "Start your story →" → High saves, shares, profile visits

Format 03 — "The Salary Reveal Reel"

"This govt job pays ₹X?"

Surprising salary number in first frame. "Most people don't know [Job Name] pays ₹47,000/month. Here's the full breakdown + how to apply before [Date]." Numbers create curiosity. Curiosity kills the scroll. → High reach, saves, website clicks

Facebook — Specific Format Playbook

The 4 Post Types That Win on Facebook NE India

Older audience, slower scroll, high trust requirement. These formats consistently get shares which = free reach.

Format 01 — "The Job Alert Post"

Full text, all details, deadline bold, bilingual CTA. Written like a helpful friend would write it, not like a corporate post.

Format 02 — "The Parent Address Post"

Directly to parents: "If you have a child between 20–30, share this. [3 govt jobs opening this week]." Gets mass forwarded to family groups.

Format 03 — "The Deadline Panic Post"

"⚠️ 72 ঘণ্টা বাকী আছে। (72 hours left.) [Job name] — এতিয়াই apply কৰক।" Urgency in local language hits harder and feels more genuine.

Format 04 — "The Weekly Roundup"

Every Sunday: "This week's top 10 government job openings in NE India." Full list with details. Gets saved, bookmarked, and forwarded. Builds habit of checking your page.

07 — Hook Formula Library

The First 3 Seconds
Are Everything

On Instagram, you have 1.5 seconds before the swipe. On Facebook, you have one opening line before the "See more" cutoff. These hook formulas are engineered to stop that scroll.

Instagram Reel — Opening Frame
"90% of [audience] get this wrong."
Example: "90% of APSC aspirants don't know about the negative marking rule. Do you?" → Reel explains it. High watch time guaranteed.
Facebook Post — Opening Line
"[Emotional opener] + immediate value."
Example: "আপোনাৰ সন্তান চাকৰি বিচাৰিছেনে? এই সপ্তাহত Assam চৰকাৰে 342 খন পদৰ বাবে আবেদন মাগিছে।" (Is your child looking for a job? This week Assam govt invited applications for 342 posts.)
Universal — Curiosity Gap
"This [job/fact/salary] surprised me."
Example: "This government job in Meghalaya pays ₹48,000/month, requires only a 10th pass, and has barely 200 applicants. I'm surprised more people don't know about it."
Urgency — All Platforms
"[Number] days left. [Specific consequence]."
Example: "3 days left. If you miss this Assam Police deadline, the next recruitment is 2 years away. Apply today." Specific. Real stakes. Not manufactured urgency.
Empathy — IG Stories + FB
"If [painful situation], this is for you."
Example: "If you've been preparing for 3+ years and still haven't gotten a call letter — this job has no written exam. Just an interview. It's for you."
Comparison — Instagram
"Stop using [bad thing]. Try [good thing]."
Example: "Stop refreshing 6 government websites every day. Let NE Career Hub send you an alert the moment a job appears. It's free. No ads." Then demo the filter feature.

The best hook you can write is one where your audience thinks: "Wait — this is exactly about me." Make it specific. Make it local. Make it feel like you wrote it just for them.
