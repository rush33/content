Foundation Week — Introduce the Person Behind the Platform
Days 1–7 · Goal: Make your audience know who you are, not just what you post
Day 1 Mon
Post 1 · AM IG Reel Awareness
Origin Story — "Why I Built NE Career Hub"
Your face on camera, 45–60 sec. Tell the real story: what problem you saw, what made you angry enough to build something. End: "I built it so no one in NE India misses their shot." This is your most important post of the entire 30 days.
Angle: The builder who refused to accept the status quo
Post 2 · PM FB Long Text Awareness
"I built NE Career Hub because I was frustrated" — Facebook personal note
300–400 word personal post. Write it like a letter to your community. Why you started, what you've built, what you want for NE India's job seekers. End with "Share this if you know someone who's still looking."
Angle: Local builder with a local mission — one of us

Angle: Useful intelligence delivered beautifully
Post 2 · PM LI Article Authority
"NE India Has a Career Information Gap — and I Built Something About It"
LinkedIn founder introduction post. 400–500 words. Professional but personal. Establish your position as someone who identified a real regional problem and engineered a solution. Tag relevant NE organizations.

⏰ Best Posting Times

Instagram Reels: 7–9pm. Instagram Carousels: 8–10am. Facebook job posts: 9–10am and 7–8pm. LinkedIn: Tuesday–Thursday 8–10am. Saturday deadline posts: 10am sharp.

🔁 Recurring Formats (Keep These Forever)

Saturday deadline alert, Sunday weekly digest, weekly quiz reel, monthly success story, state spotlight rotation. These become rituals. Rituals build audiences that show up without being asked.

📊 What to Track Weekly

IG: Reel reach, carousel saves, profile visits after personal posts. FB: Post shares, link clicks, comment volume. LinkedIn: Post impressions, connection requests from new industries. All platforms: website sign-ups mentioning social as source.

🎯 The One Rule Above All Others

Never miss the Saturday deadline post. Never skip the Sunday digest. These two rituals, done consistently for 6 months, will do more for your audience growth than any single viral moment ever could. Consistency is the strategy.
